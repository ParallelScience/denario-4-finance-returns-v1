The persistent performance gap between factor-based covariance estimators and Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage arises because standard factor models assume static factor loadings ($B_t$) over the estimation window, failing to account for the "rotation" of loadings during GARCH-driven volatility regimes. I hypothesize that a **Regime-Switching Factor-Shrinkage Hybrid** will outperform the standard Ledoit-Wolf target. Specifically, by using a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) to classify the market into "High-Volatility/High-Correlation" and "Low-Volatility/Low-Correlation" regimes, we can dynamically adjust the shrinkage intensity of the idiosyncratic matrix ($\Psi_t$) and the factor loadings ($B_t$). Instead of a uniform shrinkage target, we will apply a regime-dependent shrinkage intensity: higher shrinkage toward the Ledoit-Wolf target during high-volatility regimes (where factor loadings are unstable) and higher reliance on the factor-based structure during low-volatility regimes (where systematic signals are clearer). This approach addresses the "feedback loop" by explicitly acknowledging that the optimal covariance estimator is not a single static model, but a time-varying blend conditioned on the latent volatility state of the panel.
