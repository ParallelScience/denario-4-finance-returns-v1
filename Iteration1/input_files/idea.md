Title: Dynamic Factor-Covariance Recovery under GARCH-Induced Heteroskedasticity

This research evaluates the efficacy of a rolling-window PCA-based covariance estimator against a GARCH-adjusted Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage target within the provided 1,000-day panel. By employing a 60-day rolling window, we extract latent market and sector factors to reconstruct the covariance matrix, specifically testing whether this factor-based approach outperforms shrinkage when the high-volatility, GARCH-driven idiosyncratic noise of TSLA obscures systematic signals. We quantify the performance gap by measuring the tracking error and realized variance of Minimum Variance Portfolios (MVP) constructed under both regimes. The study identifies the precise threshold of idiosyncratic volatility where factor-based reconstruction fails due to estimation error in the latent factor loadings. By integrating GARCH(1,1) conditional variance forecasts into the shrinkage target, we demonstrate how adaptive weighting of asset-specific shocks mitigates the instability inherent in standard covariance estimation for high-beta equities.
