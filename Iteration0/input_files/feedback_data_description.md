The dataset description explains the observed limitations in the analysis. Specifically, the "Data Generating Process" defines TSLA as having GARCH(1,1) volatility clustering with high idiosyncratic noise (up to 3.0% volatility). Because the research plan relies on a 60-day rolling window to estimate latent factors and covariance, the high-volatility, time-varying nature of TSLA's idiosyncratic shocks—as explicitly constrained by the dataset's generating process—directly limits the stability of the PCA-based factor extraction. The "estimation error in the latent factor loadings" mentioned in the research idea is a direct consequence of the high idiosyncratic-to-systematic variance ratio inherent in the synthetic data for assets like TSLA, which obscures the systematic signals within the short 60-day estimation window.
