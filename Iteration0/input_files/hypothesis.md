The current factor-based covariance estimation assumes a static relationship between assets and latent factors within the 60-day rolling window. However, the GARCH-induced heteroskedasticity suggests that the "true" factor loadings (betas) are likely time-varying and sensitive to volatility regimes. I hypothesize that a "Volatility-Adjusted Factor Loading" (VAFL) approach—where factor loadings are estimated via Weighted Least Squares (WLS) using the inverse of the GARCH-forecasted conditional variance as weights—will significantly outperform standard PCA-based loadings. By giving less weight to high-volatility, noisy observations (like TSLA during spikes) during the factor extraction process, we can stabilize the latent factor recovery and reduce the turnover of the Minimum Variance Portfolio. This approach will be validated by comparing the weight stability (turnover) and realized variance of the VAFL-based covariance matrix against the standard PCA-based and Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage benchmarks, specifically testing if the VAFL method raises the idiosyncratic volatility threshold at which factor-based reconstruction typically fails.
